Move-Out Cleaning Checklist for Victoria BC Renters

With rental prices in Victoria as high as they are, your damage deposit represents a significant sum. Losing any part of it to cleaning deductions is frustrating — and usually preventable.

BC's Residential Tenancy Act requires landlords to return your deposit within 15 days of the tenancy end date unless they have deductions to claim. The most common deduction? Cleaning. Here's exactly what Victoria landlords expect and how to give yourself the best possible chance of a full return.

The BC Standard for "Reasonably Clean"

Under BC tenancy law, you're required to return the property in the same condition it was rented — normal wear and tear excepted. "Reasonably clean" in practice means:

  • All surfaces wiped, including hard-to-reach areas
  • Kitchen appliances cleaned inside and out
  • Bathroom fully sanitized — no soap scum, mould, or lime buildup
  • Floors swept, vacuumed and mopped
  • Windows cleaned (inside at minimum)
  • All cupboards and drawers emptied and wiped
  • No lingering odours

Room-by-Room Move-Out Checklist

Kitchen:

  • Oven interior — racks, bottom, door glass, under elements
  • Stovetop and burners
  • Range hood, filter and fan
  • Refrigerator interior, drawers, seals, and top
  • Microwave inside and out
  • Dishwasher (run a cleaning cycle, wipe door seal)
  • All cupboards and drawers inside and out
  • Sink, faucet and surrounding area
  • Countertops and backsplash

Bathroom(s):

  • Toilet inside, outside, around the base
  • Shower/bath walls — grout, fixtures, and glass
  • Sink and taps
  • Vanity cupboards inside and out
  • Floor, baseboards and behind toilet
  • Mirror
  • Exhaust fan (remove cover and clean)

Bedrooms & Living Areas:

  • Vacuum all carpets thoroughly (including under furniture)
  • Mop hard floors
  • Wipe all baseboards
  • Clean window sills, tracks and glass
  • Light switches and door handles wiped
  • Closets emptied and wiped
