How Often Should You Detail Your Car in Victoria BC?

One of the most common questions we get from Victoria drivers is simple: how often should I actually detail my car? The honest answer is that it depends — but there are some clear guidelines that make it easy to figure out for your specific situation.

The Short Answer

For most Victoria drivers, a full interior detail 1–2 times per year and an exterior detail every 3–4 months is a solid baseline. But your actual needs may be higher or lower depending on several factors.

Factors That Mean You Should Detail More Often

  • Kids or pets in the vehicle. Pet hair, crumbs, stains and odours accumulate fast. For households with dogs or young children, interior detailing every 3–4 months makes a real difference.
  • You park outside. Vehicles parked outside in Victoria are exposed to tree sap, bird droppings, pollen and rain deposits. Exterior details every 6–8 weeks keep paintwork protected.
  • Coastal location. Living in Oak Bay, Sidney, or Esquimalt means genuine salt air exposure. This oxidises paint and corrodes metal faster. More frequent exterior attention is worthwhile.
  • You drive for work or rideshare. High passenger volume means faster interior wear. A detail every 2–3 months maintains a professional appearance.
  • You're selling the vehicle. A proper detail before listing your vehicle can add hundreds to its perceived value.

A Simple Schedule for Most Victoria Drivers

  • Every 3 months: Exterior wash and light interior vacuum
  • Every 6 months: Full exterior detail (clay bar, polish, sealant)
  • Once a year: Full interior detail (deep clean, shampoo, deodorise)
  • As needed: Stain removal, pet hair treatment, paint correction
